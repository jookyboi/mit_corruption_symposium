Citizens Rising Conference

Friday, Sep 19, 2014 - Cambridge, MA Register

The US political system is broken; our governance has been bought and sold. We all know this, but you can change it.

Citizens Rising is a symposium exploring how this corrupted system is used to enhance the leverage of the elite few over the many, and more importantly, it's the chance to ask yourself, “What am I going to do about it?”

Speakers


We need to take our democracy back.
Lawrence Lessig
Professor of Law at Harvard Law School, Director of the Safra Center for Ethics at Harvard University, and political activist, Lawrence Lessig transformed intellectual-property law when he co-founded Creative Commons. It was during his efforts to improve copyright and cyberlaw that he ran head-long into the power of money in Congress. Now Lessig aims to realize a more fundamental transformation: fixing the system of corruption in American politics. He has founded the activist organization Rootstrikers, and launched MayDay PAC, a crowd-funded “super PAC to destroy all super PACs”. His latest book, Republic, Lost, was a major source of inspiration for this symposium.


Average citizens have little or no independent influence
Martin Gilens
Professor of Politics at Princeton University, Martin Gilens’ research includes investigating how the wealthy have undue sway on American politics. Along with Benjamin Page of Northwestern University, Gilens authored the recent paper Testing Theories of American Politics: Elites, Interest Groups, and Average Citizens which went viral in the media, being cited as evidence that the U.S. is an oligarchy. Their findings show that average citizens have little or no influence on policy outcomes. When economic elites and business groups disagree with the public majority, legislators pass policy favoring the affluent. This study, along with Gilens’ latest book,Affluence and Influence, was a major factor in motivating this symposium.
